A Study of Bitcoin De-Anonymization: Graph and Multidimensional Data Analysis

(with Ye Zhong,Qingfeng Tan)
Accepted by the IEEE DSC 2020

Abstract | Paper

Bitcoin was designed to be a decentralized global electronic payment system that does not require verification by a third-party intermediary platform and can be used by anyone originally. Due to its anonymity and globalization, bitcoin has achieved great success and attracted the attention of various illegal traders. In recent years, the number of illegal transactions of bitcoin has been increasing. Although bitcoin can support a certain amount of privacy, the bitcoin users and entity information can be linked by tracking the on-chain information of bitcoin users and combining the public off-chain information. Through bitcoin users de-anonymization, we can obtain some valuable intelligence information, which plays an important role in combating bitcoin-related crimes. In this paper, we build a visual analysis system for bitcoin transactions based on a graph database and use real-world multi-dimensional data sources to analyze the entity information of bitcoin transactions on the chain to achieve the effect of de-anonymization. Besides, we adopt a supervised learning method in our system to predict the legitimacy of unknown bitcoin transactions. Experiments and analyses show that our system can achieve good correlation analysis and de-anonymization. Finally, we put forward the future research direction of the bitcoin de-anonymization field.

Blockchain-based Proxy Re-Encryption Scheme for Data sharing in Internet of Vehicles

Xingyu Lv, Student Member, IEEE, Qing Feng Tan, Member, IEEE, Le Wang, Zhihong Tian, Member, IEEE, Xiaojiang Du, Fellow, IEEE and Mohsen Guizani, Fellow, IEEE)
Summbit to IEEE Consumer Electronics Magazine

Abstract | Paper

With the development of Internet of Vehicles(IoV) ，the value of vehicle data has received more and more attention and it cannot be fully utilized without data circulation and sharing. However, due to problems such as data security, and low quality, the sharing of vehicular data grows slowly. This paper proposes a user-centric vehicular data sharing scheme based on consortium blockchain and proxy re-encryption to solve the problem of data silos among current vehicles, vehicular service providers, IoT devices, and other beneficiaries. Besides, we built a decentralized vehicular data market to help implement federated learning to achieve more accurate vehicle insurance pricing. During the training process, participants only exchanged intermediate calculation results without revealing other data to protect vehicular data privacy and improved the quality of smart vehicular services meantime. A large number of experiments and security analysis show that the scheme can achieve trusted identity authentication in data sharing of IoV, and effectively protect the user's data privacy, while at the same time reaching the actual business scenario-level time delay.

FIOV: the Future Internet of Vehicles based on Blockchain

Accepted by CNCI 2020

Abstract | Paper

With the development of information technology, the emergence of the IOT (Internet of Things) can connect objects through intelligent terminals , and connecting everything becomes a new development trend. As one of the most important transportation for human beings, the IOV(Internet of Vehicles) which is composed of vehicles has naturally become the main application field of the IOT. However, the current development of IOV is still not perfect, there are still many problems such as users data privacy, lacking of uniform standards of IOV management and so on. With the constant iteration of technology, these issues are likely to be resolved in the near future . To this end, we propose the concept of the FIOV(Future Internet of Vehicles) , and integrate the emerging blockchain technology for the problems existing in the current IOV, so that vehicles and their infrastructure can form a P2P(point-to-point) network to communicate. In this way, the IOV becomes more free and open, and even accesses third-party services, facilitating information transmission and friendly communication between vehicles and the natural space, which is the vision of the FIOV .

An Efficient Searchable Aggregated-indexing-based Encryption Scheme

Published in COMPUTER TECHNOLOGY AND DEVELOPMENT

Abstract | Paper

The development of cloud storage technology enables users to enjoy more powerful cloud storage services. At the same time,users want to preserve the security of stored data as much as possible. Searchable encryption is an important technical means to guarantee user data security and priv-acy in cloud computing scenarios. In addition to providing sufficient security,the searchable encryption scheme for cloud storage should also have better query performance and the ability to dynamically update indexes. The existing research work is usually based on the idea of reverse indexing to optimize query performance and realize the update of index ciphertext structure. We propose a novel aggregated-index-based searchable encryption scheme, which realizes the fast positioning from keywords to file addresses,by dedicated aggregated indexing table. The experiment shows that the proposed solution has obvious advantages over the classical reverse index scheme in query efficiency. At the same time,it also provides better dynamic index update ability and security strength.

A Blockchain-based Identity Authentication Protocol for Edge IoT Devices Management

Submit to IEEE Transactions on Green Communications and Networking

Abstract | Paper

With the widespread application of edge IoT devices, the Internet of everything has become a reality. It has profoundly affected this digital economy era and brought great convenience to people's lives. However, security incidents involving the leakage of user-sensitive information due to uncontrolled access rights in edge IoT systems have occurred frequently in recent years. Besides, users need to use different authentication methods when using edge devices from different vendors, which also increases the user's burden. These problems bring great threats and challenges to the development of the IoT ecosystem. Therefore, how to design a more sustainable and smart digital identity authentication mechanism for edge IoT devices management is still a problem worthy of research. In this paper, we have proposed and implemented a blockchain-based identity authentication protocol for edge IoT devices to deal with the security threats of the existing centralized identity authentication model and solve the problem of low authentication efficiency of existing heterogeneous devices, which makes the management of edge devices more intelligent. Our experiments and performance analysis show the merits of our proposed protocol in terms of security and efficiency.
